As part of a 5-person group project, I led the design of a chemostat. By precisely controlling how fast nutrient broth is added and removed from a culture of bacteria, a chemostat can cause a colony to grow at an effectively linear rate.

The chemostat uses a peristaltic pump to insert sterile broth into a living bacteria colony and a vacuum to continuously remove the bacteria, creating an effect that limits the growth of the bacteria to a constant rate. Chemostats allow us to research the metabolisms and mutations of certain bacteria, as well as how limiting specific nutrients affects bacterial growth.

The chemostat was used by other students in the Experimental Biology Class at Nueva High School.
